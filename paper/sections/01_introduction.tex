\section{Introduction}

The previous paper, \emph{Lift \& Twist}, reached a local theorem plateau.  It showed that the four-state answer-cell target is not merely a table of observed rows.  Within the reduced target, the local data are generated from two bits: a shell bit \(b\) and a rank bit \(r\).

That result leaves a sharper question.  If the local cell is generated, does the reduced universe follow?

The reduced shared\_B universe contains 16 candidates.  In earlier work this universe was formed by crossing four C-cycles with four anchor-cycles.  The selected candidates were
\[
    [1,6,8,15].
\]
The visible selector looked twisted:
\[
    [1,2,0,3].
\]
But after lifting to shell/rank coordinates, the selector became identity.

This paper turns that observation into a derivation target:
\[
    \text{local cell} \longrightarrow \text{two readouts} \longrightarrow 16\ \text{visible candidates}.
\]
