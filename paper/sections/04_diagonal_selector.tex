\section{The hidden diagonal}

The reduced universe is the product
\[
    U = C(L)\times A(L).
\]
Index candidates by
\[
    i = 4c+a,
\]
where \(c\) is the C-readout row and \(a\) is the anchor-readout column.

The hidden diagonal is the identity condition
\[
    x_C = x_A.
\]
Since
\[
    C(L)=[O0,O1,B0,B1]
\]
and
\[
    A(L)=[B0,O0,O1,B1],
\]
the diagonal appears in visible coordinates as
\[
    O0: 0\mapsto 1,\quad
    O1: 1\mapsto 2,\quad
    B0: 2\mapsto 0,\quad
    B1: 3\mapsto 3.
\]
Therefore the visible selector is
\[
    [1,2,0,3]=(0\ 1\ 2)(3),
\]
and the candidate indices are
\[
    [1,6,8,15].
\]
